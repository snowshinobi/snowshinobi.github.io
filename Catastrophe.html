Catastrophe

You are a poem

a sneaky idea

that crawls through the room

and sleeps past noon

and noisily grooms while we think through

 

Why it's so important

that the sky is blue

and the sea full of fish

that skitter and swoon

and get caught on the claws

of poems like you

so you can leave them

on your favorite one's shoes

and she'll hoist you up

with rough hands and a croon

and decide, next time

that she'll walk down with you

to the edge of the water

on a bright afternoon

and teach you to watch

instead of harpoon

and as you half-listen

her hands shiver and smooth

out the ripples of skin

that collect around you

when you sprawl out like this

as you often do

 

because you are a poem

a sneaky idea

that crawls through the room

and sleeps past noon

and kills like you were born to.
